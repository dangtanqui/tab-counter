# Privacy Policy for Tab Counter

**Last updated: December 2024**

## 1. Introduction

Tab Counter ("we", "our", or "the extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our Chrome extension.

## 2. Data Collection

**Tab Counter does NOT collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external servers.**

All functionality operates entirely within your browser.

## 3. Local Storage

The extension stores the following data **locally on your device only** using Chrome's built-in storage API:

- Tab count history and statistics
- User preferences (dark mode, language settings, alert thresholds)
- Custom tab group templates
- Tab notes you create
- Auto-close timer settings

This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third party.

## 4. Permissions Explained

The extension requests the following permissions:

| Permission | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| `tabs` | To count, search, sort, and manage your browser tabs |
| `storage` | To save your settings and preferences locally |
| `notifications` | To show alerts when you have too many tabs open |
| `tabGroups` | To manage Chrome's built-in tab groups feature |
| `bookmarks` | To enable the "bookmark all tabs" feature |

## 5. Data Sharing

We do **NOT**:
- Collect any personal information
- Track your browsing history
- Share any data with third parties
- Use analytics or tracking services
- Display advertisements

## 6. Data Security

Since all data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's secure storage API, your data is protected by your device's security measures.

## 7. Children's Privacy

Our extension does not knowingly collect any information from children under 13 years of age.

## 8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date at the top of this document.

## 9. Your Rights

You can delete all extension data at any time by:
1. Right-clicking the extension icon
2. Selecting "Remove from Chrome"
3. Or clearing browser data in Chrome settings
